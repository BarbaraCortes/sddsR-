2010
SAE AeroDesign East
 
  EESC-USP Advanced

This plane took part of a newly created category of the AeroDesign East competition: Advanced Class. This new category replaced the Open Class, but it kept the last’s main characteristics: there weren’t dimensional restrictions and different options of propulsion were allowed, since the total cylinder displacement didn’t exceed .65 cubic inches.

The great surprise was the inclusion of a new line of project in the teams, which had to develop a data acquisition system (DAS) in order to measure the take-off distance of the airplane.

The proper operation of the DAS was fundamental to the success in the competition, so it represented a great challenge to the team, whose members are, in its majority, students of Aeronautical Engineering. So, the team decided to re-employ (with few modifications) the project EESC-USP East 2009, but it included the DAS in the airplane. The project and the construction of the DAS were held as responsibility of a single member, student of Mechatronics Engineering.

The DAS construction delayed, but in the competition’s eve it seemed to the team that the system was completely ready to make accurate measurements.

In the 1st Day of competition, the accuracy of the DAS was measured in a simple test. The airplane was pushed in the soil between two landmarks, and then the indicated distance was compared to the real distance. In this test, the DAS reached an accuracy factor of 95.3%, similar to the other teams’.

In the flight batteries, however, problems occurred in the system. In the first attempts, the DAS didn’t work. On the other hand, the airplane’s performance proved to be a lot better than the opponents’. The airplane EESC-USP Advanced flew many batteries with the maximum payload, 39.7lb (18kg), while the highest payload of the other teams was 28lb (12.7kg).

After some attempts, the DAS finally worked and the team obtained a valid flight with maximum payload. However, the distance measured was much different from the runway length, what affected the total score.

Finally, the team obtained the 2nd Place Overall, and it still obtained the awards of 1st Place Most Payload and 3rd Place Oral Presentation.
 
EESC-USP Regular

The project for the Regular Class brought several innovations for the team. The regulation of this category prohibits the usage of fiber reinforced plastics (FRPs), it offer bonus score for the airplanes which perform an empty flight and it doesn’t account structural efficiency in the total score, focusing the payload carried. In this competition, the teams must use the engine OS .61, and there is also a dimensional restriction.

In its previous project, EESC-USP Bravo 2009, the team employed an airfoil based on Selig 1223 in the wing. The last presents an extremely thin trailing edge, what complicates its construction and also diminishes the wing stiffness.  The team avoided these problems modifying the airfoil trailing edge while keeping its aerodynamics qualities, and as result the airplane presented a good performance.

After wind tunnel tests, the team proved the advantage of this airfoil compared to others already used, like Eppler 423 and EESC-1, and it was re-employed in the wing of the airplane EESC-USP Regular. Next, new materials to replace the FRPs were researched. That study revealed to the team several materials that allow an accurate construction and low empty weight, like aeronautical aluminum (which can be machined), wood sheets and PVC foams (which can be laser cut), etc.

 

In the project competition, the team was disappointed with the judges’ valuation of the Technical Report and the Oral Presentation, which are always well labored by the team and also well rated by the judges. However, in this competition the team started in 22nd place, with a disadvantage of 10 points compared to the first positioned.

In the flight batteries, however, the airplane proved the project’s quality. In the first battery, the airplane performed an empty flight and it obtained 10 bonus points. In the following batteries, the team increased the payload at each new attempt until the last battery, when the plane lifted 31.53lb (14.3kg) of payload. The team didn’t expect that performance, and the bad payload prediction cost 7 points for the team.

Finally, the team finished in 3rd Place Overall and also obtained the award of 2nd Place Most Payload.
 
  EESC-USP Micro

The airplane EESC-USP Micro marks the 1st participation of the team in the Micro Class in the AeroDesign East. The biggest challenge forced by the Rules of this category is to design an aircraft of high Payload Fraction, which fits entirely (with the radio transmitter) into a case of dimensions 18 x 13 x 5.5 in and which still admits a minimum volume in the cargo bay.

Aiming to comply with the restriction successfully, the team focused in the design of a flexible cargo bay and of joints that permitted the disassembling of the plane. The team achieved an airplane which, as seen in the picture, presents a central rod where are fixed the engine, the landing gear, the cargo bay, the wing, the elevator and the rudder. The interfaces allowed the disassembling of each part, safe the elevator, since its disassembling wouldn’t reduce the volume occupied in the case.

Besides, the wing and the central rod were split in 3 parts each; the cargo bay was located into a fairing with flexible joints and laterals, without structural function, so that it could acquire the shape of a plate when pressed. Even a tiny radio transmitter was chosen to explore all the available space in the transport case.

In the project competition, the team achieved the 1st place. In each flight battery the team increased the payload lifted, and it preserved the leadership with clearance. The last flight had a Payload Fraction of 0.745.

Finally, the team obtained the 1st Place Overall, and still got the awards of 1st Place Highest Payload Fraction, 1st Place Oral Presentation and 2nd Place Written Design Report.
 
SAE AeroDesign Brazil
 
 
EESC-USP Charlie

In the sixth participation of the team in the Open Class, the project aimed the capability of transporting the maximum weight allowed by the Rules:  77.16lb (35kg) of MTOW. The main innovations of the airplane were the employment of 2 Rossi .60 engines and of a new airfoil designed by the team.

In the project competition, the team reached the 1st place in the Technical Report and the Oral Presentation, what already gave us two honorable mentions.

In the Friday, first day of flights, the team had problems with the 2.4GHz receptors, what impeded us of flying in the first classificatory battery.

On the Saturday morning, after the problems were fixed, the airplane flew easily with 49.8lb (22.6kg) of payload, classifying the team to the next batteries. In the afternoon of the same day, the Charlie tried to fly with 55.3lb (25.1kg) of payload. But due to a strong rain that felt, the airplane couldn’t take-off.

 

In the first attempt of Sunday, the airplane flew with the maximum weight (77.16lb of MTOW), what caused strong excitement of all the team members! However, it didn’t stop inside a 200ft section of the runway and so it lost an important bonus.

Since the victory in the competition demanded the airplane stop inside a 200ft section, we tried two more times. In the first, the airplane hit an obstacle in the landing, and the flight was invalidated. In the second, the airplane didn’t take-off.

In the end of the competition, the team was in 2nd Place Overall, but took all the honorable mentions of the Open Class:
- Best Project
- Best Oral Presentation
- Best Payload Prediction
- Highest Payload
 
  EESC-USP Alpha

The airplane Alpha 2010 was an improvement of the airplane EESC-USP Regular 2010, designed to the international competition AeroDesign East 2010.

Basically, the team redesigned the structures of that airplane, diminished its dimensions, introduced a brake mechanism and also designed new interfaces that permitted the complete disassembling of the plane and its arrangement into a low volume box.

Even with the reduced time of Project, the team reached the 3rd position in the first phase of the competition, which accounted the Technical Report and the Oral Presentation.

With the result, the team went to the next day prepared to risk soon in the first flight attempt, for which the plane was loaded with 28.66lb (13kg) of payload. However, problems in the landing gear impeded the take-off. For the next attempt, the team planned to fly with lower payload, to guarantee the classification. Then, the airplane was loaded with only 22.04lb (10kg), and it flew easily. However, the wheel detached from the main landing gear in the landing, and the flight was invalidated.

 After the two failures, the team prepared to the next flight, since it would be the last chance of classification. The airplane was loaded with 15.43lb (7kg), it took-off, it flew and it landed without any problem. The classification of the team provoked great commemoration of the members.

Still in the Saturday afternoon, the team had a new flight attempt, which failed due to a strong rain that reached the runway at the moment. Then, the most loaded flights were all postponed to the last day of competition.

In the Sunday, the team started with 27.56lb (12.5kg) of payload, with success. Next, the airplane flew with 28.97lb (13.14kg). In the landing, the airplane could stop exactly in the limit of the runway, and this battery score left the team leading the competition! This achievement caused an euphoric commemoration of all members of the EESC-USP Team.

Then, the organizers opened a new battery for the 10 best placed team of the Regular Class. The Alpha team planned to increase the payload lifted, intending to beat a possible increase of the others teams’ scoring.  In the battery, the Tucano team, from UFU, flew with nearly 31lb (14kg) and it still obtained the bonus for stopping in the landing.

The flight of the Alpha team was extremely exciting. After the take-off, the airplane hardly gained altitude and it flew few centimeters from the ground. After some while, the plane could reach some meters of altitude, and then approached to land. In the landing, the airplane touched the wheels in the beginning of the runway, it bounced, and it returned to the ground 100ft (30.5m) forward, impeding the stop before the limit of 200ft (61m).

Then, the team Alpha 2010 finished in 2nd Place Overall, after the winner Tucano, from UFU, and both teams will represent Brazil in the Regular Class of the AeroDesign East competition.
