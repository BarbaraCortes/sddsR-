The L.S. Starrett Company was founded 1880 in Athol, MA, which is still the Company's World Headquarters. Our founder, Laroy S. Starrett invented the combination square in 1877. He started the company to manufacture this product. The company grew rapidly with a broadening product line, additional employees and penetration into an increasing number of markets. Starrett has a history and reputation so rich and solid that the words "quality" and "Starrett" have become virtually synonymous.
 

Alltec Composite Materials is a company from Sao Jose dos Campos that works specifically with the manufacturing of components in composite materials, moulds and thermal conformation of plastics for aeronautical and aerospace industrial applications.

Alltec is the team's oldest partner, giving support to the Brazilian teams since the year of 2000. Their support is crucial for keeping the high structural performance of our teams' aircraft and for our learning about handling and manufacturing parts in composite materials.
 
 
 

CD-adapco is the world's largest independent CFD focused provider of engineering simulation software, support and services. We have over 30 years of experience in delivering industrial strength engineering simulation. The scope of our activities extends well beyond software development to encompass a wide range of CAE engineering services in both CFD and FEA.
 

The Aeronautics, in the USP Sao Carlos, began in 1973 with the formation of a group for the design and construction of an aircraft for basic instruction. Part of this group, in 1978, was responsible for the creation of the Aircraft’s Laboratory, LAE, in the Mechanical Engineering Department, Engineering School of Sao Carlos.

With the creation of the Aeronautical Engineering course in USP, and the consequent expansion of the research lines, the NPA, Nucleus for Research in Aeronautics, was formed. As well as offering disciplines with aerospace content to the course Aeronautical Engineering, the nucleus also assists the national aviation industry through the development of research in partnership with corporations such as Embraer.

The department has always provided structural and technical support to the team, especially concerning the installation of our workshop and availability of the wind tunnels for our aerodynamic tests.
